Relaxing wearable

Neurospace: A wearable for the neck that gives you relaxing massages to the rythm of a song made just for you, to relax you, thanks to machine learning.

It massages the right spots of the back of the user's head and neck to induce a relaxed state. It simultaneously plays relaxing music through ergonomic headphones. This music is composed with help from the machine learning tool "Magenta" which analyzes preference data given by the Tensor Flow and produces a unique musical piece accommodating to each user. Neurospace will massage its user to the beat of the music it is playing, utilizing heart beat data to determine the right rythm. It will also monitor the user's stress levels and learn what music is more effective. It also serves as a health database because it measures and displays heart rate through a light.

Massage Therapy

The National Institutes of Health has advised that massage therapy can reduce fatigue and improve sleep. A study on back pain, published in the International Journal of Neuroscience , demonstrated that subjects receiving massage experienced improved sleep and an increase in serotonin levels, which is essential for the production of melatonin. As melatonin influences the sleep stage of our circadian rhythm, a natural way of boosting serotonin is a positive sleep inducing option.

Musical Relaxation

Music is effective for relaxation and stress management. Research confirms these personal experiences with music. Current findings indicate that music around 60 beats per minute can cause the brain to synchronize with the beat causing alpha brainwaves (frequencies from 8 - 14 hertz or cycles per second). This alpha brainwave is what is present when we are relaxed and conscious. To induce sleep (a delta brainwave of 5 hertz), a person may need to devote at least 45 minutes, in a relaxed position, listening to calming music. Researchers at Stanford University (press release 2006) have said that "listening to music seems to be able to change brain functioning to the same extent as medication."
